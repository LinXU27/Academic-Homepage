About

I completed both my undergraduate and postgraduate studies at the School of Public Health, Xiamen University, where my research has focused on antibody-drug conjugates (ADCs). I have participated in multiple research projects and independently led a TF-targeted ADC project. My technical expertise includes drug screening, molecular design, and in vitro and in vivo efficacy evaluation.
To date, I have published seven SCI Q1 papers, with one co-authored manuscript under review, and have contributed to three patents. I was also awarded the National Scholarship.
In addition, I gained valuable clinical experience through prior work in a hospital and an internship in Pfizer’s drug product department, which strengthened my understanding of translational research and pharmaceutical development.
I am responsible, collaborative, and quick to learn, with strong problem-solving and communication skills.

Research Interests

Antibody-Drug Conjugates (ADCs)

ADCs are a class of therapeutics in which a monoclonal antibody is chemically linked to a small-molecule cytotoxic payload via a linker, combining the antibody’s targeting capability with the potent cell-killing activity of the cytotoxic agent.

Nanobody; Site-specific conjugation; Targeted tumor therapy
